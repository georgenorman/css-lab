Relative div nested inside static

This series of examples shows some of the wacky things that happen when you nest a relative div inside statically positioned ones. I'm not sure if there would ever be a reason to do this, but good to know, in case it's ever done by accident.

Inner-most div has no margin

There are three divs: inner-most is nested inside of middle, which in turn is nested inside of outer-most. The resulting position of the inner-most div is exactly as expected - it's relatively positioned inside of middle, at 40px from the top and 40px from the left.

Inner-most div has 10px margin

Now, lets keep everything the same, except add a margin to the inner-most div (it's relatively positioned inside two static divs). Adding a 10px margin to the inner-most div has no effect on its top position, but did effect the left position by 10px (it's now 50px from the left outer side).

Inner-most div has 40px margin

Increasing the margin of the relative div to 40px, still has no effect on its position relative to the top of its immediate parent. However, it did push down its parent and its parents parent by about 26px, as evidenced by the gap immediately above the rendered result.

Relative div nested inside absolute

Inner-most div has 40px margin

Using a relative div nested inside of an absolute one makes all of the issues of the previous section go away. The margin of the relative div is 40px and its position relative to the top of its immediate parent is the expected 80px. Furthermore, the gap at the top of the outer-most div is now gone. Adding a border to the outer-most div will not change the result (as it does if the middle div were static).
