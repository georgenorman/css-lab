Position - Static

Now, let's keep everything the same, except change the position of the middle grid from absolute to static. This causes the top margins of the inner divs to transfer to the outermost div (as evidenced by the more than 10px gap above the outermost div). This is very similar to what was described in the Positioning lab - See the Margin Transfer section for details.

Position - Static with Border

Similar to what was found in the Positioning lab, by adding a top border to an outer div, the Margin Transfer "feature" can be blocked. The example below, shows that adding a top border to middleGridEx3 protects it from the margin transfer. However, outermostEx3 is still affected by the margin transfer, as evidenced by the more than 10px gap above outermostEx3.

Summary: outermostEx3 has no border, padding or margin; middleGridEx3 has a 10px margin, position:static and a thin top border; innerBoxEx3 has a 10px margin. Current 'position' styles:

Position - Static with Padding

Adding some padding to the outermost div can also mitigate the affects of Margin Transfer, when using static divs. However, the width of the outer div, as well as the margin of the inner div, will need to be reduced, to compensate.
